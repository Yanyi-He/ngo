# COAN Branding System

## Brand Overview

Organization name: Canadian Observers and Activists Network

Short name: COAN

Brand position: A Canadian nonprofit and public-service information platform helping Chinese-speaking newcomers understand Canadian society, public services, community rules, public policy, and civic participation.

Brand personality:

- Professional
- Trustworthy
- Inclusive
- Community-focused
- Calm
- Practical
- Modern but not flashy

The COAN brand should feel like a community institution that could partner with public libraries, campuses, settlement agencies, municipal offices, and volunteer networks. It should not feel like a startup, SaaS product, crypto brand, or AI company.

## Logo Concepts

### Concept 1: Civic Window

Idea: A simple square mark containing a stylized window or document frame with a subtle maple-leaf corner. The window represents observation, public information, transparency, and access to Canadian civic life.

Best use:

- Website header
- Admin dashboard
- Resource PDFs
- Volunteer certificates

Visual direction:

- Deep Canadian blue square
- White internal window shape
- Small soft maple red accent in one corner
- Wordmark to the right: Canadian Observers and Activists Network
- Short form lockup: COAN

Why it fits: It communicates trust, public information, and structured guidance without looking political or decorative.

### Concept 2: Community Compass

Idea: A circular compass-like mark built from four simple directional shapes. The shapes represent newcomers, volunteers, public services, and civic participation moving toward shared understanding.

Best use:

- Event materials
- Volunteer program identity
- Social media avatars

Visual direction:

- Blue circle or ring
- White negative space
- Soft red north marker
- Optional bilingual tagline under the wordmark

Why it fits: It suggests navigation and orientation, which are central to newcomer support.

### Concept 3: Open Bridge

Idea: A minimal bridge or pathway mark formed by two vertical supports and a connecting arch. It represents support between newcomers and Canadian public life.

Best use:

- Partnership decks
- Grant applications
- Community outreach materials

Visual direction:

- Deep blue linework
- White background
- Red accent used only as a small connector or endpoint

Why it fits: It feels institutional, stable, and community-oriented.

### Recommended Direction

Use Concept 1, Civic Window, as the primary logo direction. It is the strongest fit for a public information portal and can scale well across website navigation, favicon, documents, and admin surfaces.

## Logo Usage Guidelines

### Primary Logo

Use the full name lockup when space allows:

Canadian Observers and Activists Network

Use this on:

- Website header on desktop
- Footer
- Partnership documents
- Presentation title slides
- Official PDFs

### Short Logo

Use COAN when space is limited.

Use this on:

- Mobile header
- Favicon
- Social avatar
- Small cards
- Admin side navigation

### Clear Space

Keep clear space around the logo equal to the height of the letter C in COAN.

Do not place the logo close to page edges, photos, busy patterns, or other organization logos.

### Minimum Size

- Full horizontal logo: 180 px wide minimum on screen.
- COAN square mark: 32 px minimum on screen.
- Print: avoid using the short mark below 10 mm wide.

### Approved Color Uses

- Primary: deep blue mark with white lettering.
- Reversed: white mark on deep blue background.
- One-color: deep blue on white or light gray.
- Accessibility use: black on white when color reproduction is not reliable.

### Do Not

- Do not use gradients in the logo.
- Do not stretch or rotate the logo.
- Do not place the logo on low-contrast backgrounds.
- Do not add shadows, glows, bevels, or animation.
- Do not recolor the logo with neon, purple, orange, or dark futuristic palettes.
- Do not use the maple red as the dominant logo color.

- Avoid full-page dark themes.
- Avoid heavy gradients, glass effects, and decorative color blobs.

## Typography System

COAN typography should prioritize readability, scanning, and accessibility.

### Recommended Fonts

Primary UI font:

- Arial
- Helvetica
- system sans-serif fallback

Why: Reliable, readable, available across environments, and appropriate for a public-service website.

Optional future brand upgrade:

- Source Sans 3
- Noto Sans
- Inter

For Chinese content:

- Noto Sans SC
- Microsoft YaHei
- PingFang SC
- system sans-serif fallback

### Type Scale

| Token | Size | Line Height | Use |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| `font.size.xs` | 12 px | 18 px | Captions, metadata |
| `font.size.sm` | 14 px | 22 px | Cards, forms, nav |
| `font.size.base` | 16 px | 26 px | Body copy |
| `font.size.lg` | 18 px | 30 px | Lead text |
| `font.size.xl` | 20 px | 30 px | Small section headings |
| `font.size.2xl` | 24 px | 34 px | Section headings |
| `font.size.3xl` | 32 px | 40 px | Page headings |
| `font.size.4xl` | 40 px | 48 px | Homepage heading only |

### Typography Rules

- Use sentence case for headings whenever possible.
- Do not use negative letter spacing.
- Keep paragraphs between 60 and 80 characters per line on reading-heavy pages.
- Use bold weight for hierarchy, not oversized type.
- Avoid centered long-form text.
- Make labels clear and descriptive.

## Iconography Recommendations

Icon style:

- Simple line icons
- Rounded line caps
- 1.75 px to 2 px stroke
- No filled decorative icons unless used for status badges

Recommended icon library:

- Lucide React

Recommended icons:

- Housing: Home
- Healthcare: HeartPulse or Cross
- Tax and benefits: FileText or Landmark
- Community life: Users
- Public policy: ScrollText or Building2
- Civic engagement: Vote or Megaphone
- Student support: GraduationCap
- Volunteer: HandHeart
- Events: CalendarDays
- Chatbot: MessagesSquare
- Privacy: ShieldCheck

Icon rules:

- Icons support labels; they should not replace labels in primary navigation.
- Use icons inside action buttons only when they clarify the action.
- Do not mix filled, outlined, and illustrative icon styles.
- Keep icons aligned to a consistent 20 px or 24 px grid.
- Do not use playful, cartoon, neon, or mascot-style icons.

## Spacing System

COAN uses an 8 px spacing system with a few smaller utility values.

| Token | Value | Use |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `space.1` | 4 px | Tight inline gaps |
| `space.2` | 8 px | Small gaps |
| `space.3` | 12 px | Form and badge spacing |
| `space.4` | 16 px | Card internals, mobile gutters |
| `space.5` | 20 px | Compact panels |
| `space.6` | 24 px | Card padding |
| `space.8` | 32 px | Section internal grouping |
| `space.10` | 40 px | Page blocks |
| `space.12` | 48 px | Standard section padding |
| `space.14` | 56 px | Large section padding |
| `space.16` | 64 px | Major page rhythm |

Layout widths:

- Desktop max width: 1280 px
- Reading content width: 768 px to 1024 px
- Form width: 640 px to 900 px
- Card grids: 2 columns on tablet, 3 or 4 columns on desktop when content is short

Spacing rules:

- Use section padding consistently.
- Do not nest cards inside cards.
- Keep page sections full-width with constrained inner content.
- Use generous whitespace around important explanatory content.
- Keep form groups visually calm and predictable.

## Component Design Rules

### Buttons

Primary button:

- Deep blue background
- White text
- 4 px to 6 px radius
- Minimum height: 44 px
- Use for the main action on a page

Secondary button:

- White background
- Deep blue border
- Deep blue text
- Use for alternate actions

Subtle button:

- White or transparent background
- Neutral border
- Use for low-priority navigation

Button rules:

- Use clear verbs: Join Us, Browse resources, Register, Submit application.
- Avoid vague labels like Learn More when a clearer action exists.
- Do not use pill buttons as the default style.

### Cards

Card rules:

- 6 px to 8 px border radius.
- White background.
- Thin neutral border.
- Minimal shadow only when needed.
- Title, short description, metadata, then action.
- Cards should represent one resource, event, post, role, or admin item.

Do not:

- Put cards inside other cards.
- Use decorative cards as section backgrounds.
- Use oversized card padding for dense admin surfaces.

### Forms

Form rules:

- Every field must have a visible label.
- Use helper text for sensitive fields.
- Consent checkboxes must use clear, explicit language.
- Error messages should be specific and placed near the field.
- Required fields should be indicated consistently.

Form states:

- Default
- Focus
- Error
- Disabled
- Submitted
- Under review

### Notices

Notice types:

- Info: public information or guidance.
- Warning: disclaimer, professional-advice boundary, data sensitivity.
- Success: application submitted, registration confirmed.
- Error: submission failed or missing required information.

Notice rules:

- Use plain language.
- Keep the title short.
- Do not use alarming colors unless the issue is serious.

### Navigation

Top navigation:

- Home
- About
- Resources
- Events
- Volunteer
- Community
- AI Chatbot

Header CTA:

- Join Us

Navigation rules:

- Keep top navigation simple.
- Do not expose admin navigation as a primary public item.
- Mobile navigation should be text-first and keyboard accessible.

### Resource Cards

Required fields:

- Category
- Type
- Title
- Summary
- Source orientation

Resource card rules:

- Explain what the user will learn.
- Mention official-source pathways.
- Avoid giving definitive legal, medical, financial, tax, or immigration advice.

### Event Cards

Required fields:

- Title
- Date
- Time
- Location or online status
- Capacity
- Registration deadline
- Short description

Event card rules:

- Make logistics visible.
- Make registration action clear.
- Avoid promotional language.

### Community Posts

Community rules:

- Moderated tone.
- No anonymous hostility.
- No private sensitive details.
- Encourage practical questions and source sharing.
- Mark review or moderation status clearly.

## Accessibility Rules

- Target WCAG-inspired accessibility standards.
- Use semantic HTML.
- Preserve visible focus states.
- Maintain high contrast for text and controls.
- Use descriptive labels for inputs.
- Do not rely on color alone for meaning.
- Keep clickable targets at least 44 px tall where possible.
- Support keyboard navigation.
- Avoid motion-heavy interactions.

## Voice and Content Rules

COAN voice:

- Professional but warm
- Clear and practical
- Trustworthy and careful
- Inclusive of newcomers, students, families, and volunteers
- Source-aware

Use:

- "general information"
- "official sources"
- "community support"
- "public services"
- "civic participation"
- "newcomers"

Avoid:

- Startup-style slogans
- Overpromising
- Legal, medical, financial, tax, or immigration certainty
- Political partisanship
- Casual slang in official content
